Child Healthcare


Description: Child healthcare was a particular concern from the focus group participants. To lower costs, many families used free clinics as a main healthcare provider. However, long waits and transportation was often very difficult when children were involved. Free clinics were the lower cost option but families were concerned about their quality, long waits, and transportation costs.

Comments:

  • 'Emphasis was placed on care provided by UNHCR centers or free centers, due to the presence of large number of children.'

  • 'They do not resort to the doctor immediately (they use herbs, pain killers and children's hypothermia drugs are always available at home).'

  • 'the poor waiting experience in some places has been a source of complain, especially with the existence of children.'

  • 'Waiting in line at the Emirati Hospital after waking up very early.'
    'I always stand in line, but my children have the experience and intelligence to find what is appropriate for me so I do not wait long.'

  • 'Mobility is a burden with more than one child.'
    'The presence of a number of children makes them always vulnerable to emergencies.'

  • 'They are totally unable to go to another governorate to receive treatment even if the service was free of charge, because they have children who cannot be left at home or be taken on a treatment trip that might last a full day.'

  • 'I wish there was Health care can be provided to children and for emergency conditions.'

  • 'They show clear interest in the health of their children. They want to receive the best care, and they possess some knowledge and information that are useful when dealing with emergency cases.'
